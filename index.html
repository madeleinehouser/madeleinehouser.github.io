Color Theory

The CMY Primaries are Cyan, Magenta, and Yellow.
They are process primaries based on on the secondary colors of the RGB additive colors.


The RGB Secondaries are Red, Green, and Blue.
Mixing light wavelengths to be perceivable to the human eye's cones is done with additive primaries of Red, Green, and Blue.


Tertiary colors are the result of mixing primary colors with secondary colors.


Complementary colors are color opposites.
They are hues that are directly across from one another.


A Split Complement is a color directly across the two colors on either side of its complement.
Analogous colors are groups of 3 or 4 colors that are adjacent to each other.
